The ICON, Sting

Since the 1980's, Sting has had a very relatable and full career in wrestling. He started as Surfer Sting, adorned in vibrant, neon colors in his face paint and in his wrestling gear. Through the 1990's, he transitioned into The Crow Sting, becoming a silent, but truly feared entity, later in the 1990's, he transitioned into Wolfpac Sting, adorning red and black. After WCW, Sting became Joker Sting, and...in my humble opinion, played the Joker's "crazy" well...but, I digress. His career has spanned several entities within one character, creating an idol that shows the human side of reality, even if it is just a show. As of writing and creating this page, I, Tara Vonmacbiorne, will confidently state that as a long-time fan, Sting, aka, Steve Borden has led a legacy that has transcended time. He is a legend for all the right reasons. He was a good guy, a bad guy, an anti-hero, and an unbeatable force.


Sting started on television in the 80's. Since then, he's had the drive, the charisma, and the Stinger Splash. His vibrant, smile, along with his neon 80's Surfer look, he embodied the fresh, (look back at the 80's....it sold) sexy look everyone admired. As he grew with NWA, then turning to WCW, Sting took the world by storm. The 90's brought issues within the faction. Sting, reinvents himself, and becomes a silent, frightening force. Untill he retired, the ICON has had multipule championships and had gained admiration from millions of people, both inside and outside the ring. As the years progress, Sting has remade himself. He has evolved, and changed, but remained a foundation for others. He has also done something else, something unnoticed, except to other keen eyed spooky kids (like myself)...He made it cool to be a darker entity...a goth, or other dark/macabre/and spooky loving beings. Sting/Steve Borden made it cool to be in a sports arena. Authentic. Sting has done more than people realize to gain the nae ICON.

Personal thoughts, from one fan to another

So, this is going to be a heart pouring, but, here it goes... I have been a fan since he started in NWA. Off and on. It WAS the 80's...I had the absolute pleasure to had met this awesome human. In 2000, Miami, after Uncensored. I giggle as I write this...My mo and I were looking for her vehicle, and as we turn away, we look back, and POOF....here comes Sting. I had a black shirt, and black skirt, big stompy platforms, and....Sting facepaint. Grease Facepaint...the type you get at Halloween. I froze. Starstruck, and my mind glitching...all I could say is (looking like a deer in headlights) "Oh my God" over and over, in the fastest way possible...Mom told EVERYONE. Instead of getting annoyed, he calmly told me and my mom he was going to get in his vehicle, he would be back. As I caught my breath, I pull out a hand-drawn picture (re-drawn 25 years later, when I started this coding journey) I handed him this piece of notebook paper, and said something like "I don't want anything, I just want to give you this, its not much, but, Happy Birthday" ..... Life happened....Then I almost didn't. My life fell apart. In 2015 was the start of me picking mine up. I always had that one friend that would nudge me and randomly tell me a thing about Sting. "Did you hear Sting..." I somehow kept up, without knowing. As I have changed, and evolved personally, I have been able to look back, and now, after issing the last 25 years, I am catching up on his career, as I heal from my own path, and realize that love, and warmth in that moment, that and other major things in my life, were what kept me going. I can relate to him, in resiliance. Knowing there is still a love for an ICON, as Sting, to this day, has helped pull me back into my own authentic self...as is the coding, and other things I am taking back into my life. So, for the years of strength, showing all like me, that its cool to take your strength, and get the second wind, beat your chest, and focus, continuing the fight. Untill there's nothing left. For me, Sting has embodied the resiliance of a warrior. Aforce that, no matter what, you ALWAYS get up, and carry on with more focus, and the threshld of that old pain, raised. Give your all. Show them YOU are the force. I guess, what I really want to say is....

Thank you, Sting.
