The ICON, Sting

Since the 1980's, Sting has had a very relatable and full career in wrestling. He started as Surfer Sting, adorned in vibrant, neon colors in his face paint and in his wrestling gear. Through the 1990's, he transitioned into The Crow Sting, becoming a silent, but truly feared entity, later in the 1990's, he transitioned into Wolfpac Sting, adorning red and black. After WCW, Sting became Joker Sting, and...in my humble opinion, played the Joker's "crazy" well...but, I digress. His career has spanned several entities within one character, creating an idol that shows the human side of reality, even if it is just a show. As of writing and creating this page, I, Tara Vonmacbiorne, will confidently state that as a long-time fan, Sting, aka, Steve Borden has led a legacy that has transcended time. He is a legend for all the right reasons. He was a good guy, a bad guy, an anti-hero, and an unbeatable force.


Sting started on television in the 80's. Since then, he's had the drive, the charisma, and the Stinger Splash. His vibrant, smile, along with his neon 80's Surfer look, he embodied the fresh, (look back at the 80's....it sold) sexy look everyone admired. As he grew with NWA, then turning to WCW, Sting took the wresting world by storm...he was becoming an Icon. Through his career, his personal life was....not good. Looking back, it was his eyes. When the intensity was gone. As his character grew, he did as well personally. Anyone can respect that.
