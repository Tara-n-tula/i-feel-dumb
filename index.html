The ICON, Sting


This Is Sting!

1988-Bleach blonde Surfer guy awaits in the back. Music starts to jam. Nervous as hell, Sting sets himself up for a long career.
Since the late 1980's, Sting has had a very relatable and full career in wrestling. He started as Surfer Sting, adorned in vibrant, neon colors in his face paint and in his wrestling gear. Through the 1990's, he transitioned into The Crow Sting, becoming a silent, but truly feared entity, later in the 1990's, he transitioned into Wolfpac Sting, adorning red and black. After WCW, Sting became Joker Sting, and...in my humble opinion, played the Joker's "crazy" well...but, I digress. His career has spanned several entities within one character, creating an idol that shows the human side of reality, even if it is a show. STeve Borden created an entity the second his face hit the television. As of writing and creating this page, I, Tara Ntula, will confidently state that as a long-time fan, Sting, aka, Steve Borden has led a legacy that has transcended time. He is a legend for all the right reasons. He was a good guy, a bad guy, an anti-hero, and an unbeatable force.


Sting started on television in 1988. Since then, he's had the drive, the charisma, and the Stinger Splash. His vibrant, smile, along with his neon 80's Surfer look, he embodied the fresh, (look back at the 80's....it sold) sexy look everyone admired. As he grew with NWA, then turning to WCW, Sting took the wresting world by storm...he was becoming an Icon. Through his career, his personal life was....not good. Looking back, it was his eyes. When the intensity was gone. As his character grew, he did as well personally. Anyone can respect that.

As you see, his career has spanned 40 years and many factions. He has had multiple personnas within the Sting franchise. As he has retired, officially, first in March, then in December of 2025, the Legend has closed the book on his illustrious career, filled with oceans of love, and admiration from millions of fans, and fellow sports figures.

One more??

As with all of us, Sting has reached a point, He's done. Wrestling at his age can bring about a plethera of issues, and, I, personally believe he is tired. Maybe not of the sport, but, just because of his age. One more match? No. Definately not...no complaints here. As a fan.

Personal thoughts, from one fan to another

As one of many who was blessed (albeit 25 years ago...) to have met this awesome human, even missing out on the last 25 years of his career, and now catching up on it, it has been a pleasure, and honor to be alive in this time, to have watched Sting go from a total badass to retire as "Old Man Sting", still badass...but, It was an honor to bear witness to his career as a wrestler, and all around great dude. I do remember the look of concern, because of my barrage of "Oh My God!!" as he was approaching, even looking back on that memory now, handing him the picture I drew (the replica, redrawn 25 years later...top of page), damn near hyperventilating, was an honor to be part of his career watching his matches, the warmth of meeting him, and the feeling of belonging. Now, it is a homecoming for me, as it is full circle. A glorious full career, adoring fans, and the man behind it all, with the biggest heart for the sport, and his fans.

Thank you, Sting.
